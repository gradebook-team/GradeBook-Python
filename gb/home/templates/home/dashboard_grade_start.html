GPA standards explained

High School standard doesn't deal with + and - for grades. So a 90% still counts as an A (4.0), and not an A- (3.7). It is named this way because certain schools, including Newport, use this type of system.

Traditional standard does deal with + and - for grades. So a 90% counts as an A- (3.7). It is named that way because most places use this type of scale, including CollegeBoard.

Depending on your grade, one standard or the other will be better. For example, if all of your classes are scored 90%, your High School standard GPA would still be a 4.0, but your Traditional standard GPA would be a 3.7. On the other hand, if all your grades were 89%, your High School standard GPA would be a 3.0, while your Traditional standard GPA would be a 3.3.
