GradeBook Data Collection

In order for GradeBook to function properly, it needs to be able to store data on its database. This brief article will explain in detail the content that is stored for each user.

GradeBook is essentially a middle-man between the Edupoint servers and the client's device. When a student wants to see his or her grades, they will enter their login info, which is sent securely via HTTPS to our servers. We use these credentials to retrieve a set of cookies. These cookies are like tokens that tell the Edupoint servers that you're cool. GradeBook stores these cookies in a database, so that we can authenticate requests to Edupoint servers on the student's behalf.

The reason why GradeBook must store these cookies is that if we didn't, every time a student wanted to perform an action, they would have to enter their username and password again! In order to avoid the hassle of having to enter your password every... damn... time, we store these authentication cookies so we can submit them for you. Us storing these cookies is actually the safest option, because if someone were to (god forbid) get a hold of your cookies, they would only be able to use them to log on within a specific time-window, and in addition, the cookies do not expose your login credentials.

Cookies were the biggies of this article. GradeBook stores three other items:

  1. Class URLs
  2. Quarter URLs
  3. Profile pictures
Because GradeBook uses simplified URL schemes, we need to map our simple URLs to the complicated ones StudentVUE uses. Because of this, GradeBook will scrape your account for all the necessary links. The Class URLs are basically the URLs to view assignments for each class. The Quarter URLs are the URLs to view grades from previous or future quarters. The profile picture is mostly for aesthetic purposes, to display on the home screen. In order to speed up the loading of our webpages, we store the link to your profile picture in our database. The URLs we collect are completely unrelated to your account, so there is no risk in us storing them.

We understand if our data storage makes you feel uncomfortable. If you would like to opt out of us storing data, you can always delete your account in the settings tab. This will delete ALL data we have stored for your account.

GradeBook is trying to be as transparent as possible with what we store on our users. We hope that our transparency ultimately contributes to a trusting relationship between our users and our product.
